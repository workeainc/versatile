## Humidity is a design constraint

In Bangladesh, material selection is not only a style decision. Humidity affects wood movement, wall finish durability, metal corrosion, fabric maintenance, and how quickly surfaces show wear. This is especially important for [residential interiors](/services/residential), restaurants, and showrooms that need to age well under daily use.

## Choose stable substrates

Cabinetry and built-in furniture depend on what sits below the visible finish. A beautiful veneer or laminate will not perform well if the substrate is weak, poorly sealed, or installed without expansion planning.

## Think about maintenance from the beginning

Every surface has a maintenance personality. Matte walls, polished stone, fabric upholstery, glass partitions, and natural wood each need different care. Good design makes maintenance realistic for the people who will actually use the space.

Budget planning should account for that maintenance reality. Our guide to [residential interior design cost in Bangladesh](/blog/residential-interior-design-cost-bangladesh) explains why cheaper finishes can become expensive when they fail early.

## Balance natural and engineered materials

Natural materials add warmth and depth, but engineered finishes can offer better stability in high-use areas. The goal is not to avoid natural materials. The goal is to place them where they can age well.

## Details matter

Edges, joints, sealants, hardware, ventilation, and cleaning access often decide how long an interior feels new. Material quality matters, but detailing is what protects that investment.

## A practical rule

Use the most durable finishes where hands, water, food, sunlight, and movement are frequent. Save delicate finishes for controlled moments where they can be appreciated without being overworked.
